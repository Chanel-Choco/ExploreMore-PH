Welcome to Visayas

The Visayas is the central island group of the Philippines, made up of seven major islands (Bohol, Cebu, Leyte, Masbate, Negros, Panay, and Samar) along with countless smaller ones. When people think of the Visayas, iconic destinations like Boracay’s white sands and the Chocolate Hills often come to mind. But beyond these famous sites, the region is a natural paradise with breathtaking beaches, vibrant coral reefs, and different kinds of landforms. Visayas offers endless adventures and unforgettable experiences, where every corner reveals a new wonder waiting to be explored.

Hidden Tourist Spot in Western Visayas

Guisi Lighthouse in Guimaras


Want to explore Philippine history during the Spanish colonial era? Visit Guisi Lighthouse, also known as Faro de Punta Luzaran, in Nueva Valencia, Guimaras. It is one of the oldest lighthouses in the country, built in the 18th century. You can admire its remaining structure, nearby rock formations, and the breathtaking coastline view. Afterward, relax and enjoy the beach right next to it, where history meets beach vibes.

Location of this
Hidden Gem

Guisi, Barangay Dolores, Nueva Valencia, Guimaras, Philippines

Ideal Time and Months to Visit

Guisi Lighthouse is located in Guimaras, Philippines. The best time to visit is during April and May when the weather is sunny and good. This is an ideal opportunity to explore the lighthouse and enjoy the nearby beach. Additionally, you can visit in January, especially during the last week, to experience the Sadsaran Festival celebrated in Nueva Valencia. This festival allows you to gain a deeper appreciation of the local culture and history. It is also best if you visit it during the morning or afternoon to take good pictures of the lighthouse during sunset.

Discover Local Celebrations

Manggahan Festival

Guimaras is known as the Mango Capital of the Philippines. Every summer, they celebrate the mango harvest and give thanks to the people who grow them. If you love mangoes, try the Mango-All-You-Can challenge where you pay ₱100 to eat as many mangoes as you can in 30 minutes.

Sadsaran Festival

Held every last week of January, the word 'Sadsaran' is rooted in 'sadsad' is named after a local word for dancing in Nueva Valencia. It features cultural performances, music, and traditional dances. Since fishing is a key livelihood, the festival also highlights seafood dishes and local delicacies.

Comfortable Places to Stay

Cabrera Guest House (Formerly known as Green Bamboo Guimaras)

It is a beachfront homestay in Nueva Valencia, Guimaras. Guests can enjoy amenities like a terrace, free WiFi, private parking, and access to a shared kitchen and picnic area. Each unit or room is air-conditioned and may include a patio or dining area, making it ideal for families or groups.

Taste the Local Flavors

JaDel Food & Refreshment Hub

Jadel Food and Refreshment Hub, located beside the Municipal Hall of Nueva Valencia in Poblacion, Guimaras, offers a flavorful experience rooted in Filipino favorites. From hearty bowls of Jadel’s Signature Batchoy and sizzling Pork Sisig to the savory comfort of homemade Lumpia Shanghai and Chicken Inasal, this cozy spot celebrates local tastes. Whether you're craving a classic meal with rice or refreshing drinks and snacks, this restaurant brings a taste of Filipino hospitality in every bite.

Kusina Ingan by Leonisa's Diner

Kusina Ingan by Leonisa's Diner is a cozy eatery nestled in a 38-year-old ancestral home in Igang, Nueva Valencia, Guimaras, offering a warm and homey dining experience. They serve a wide variety of drinks, including coffee-based beverages, frappes, milkshakes, teas, juices, and refreshing fresh fruit shakes. The menu also features burgers, shawarma, nachos, sandwiches, and a selection of street foods like kikiam, lumpiang shanghai, and more.

Safety Assessment

The Guisi Lighthouse is a typically safe place to visit, but because it's on a hilltop, it's best to wear proper shoes for the steep and uneven slopes. You should also avoid going when it's raining because the pathways may get slippery. Because the lighthouse is an old structure, pay attention to your surroundings and keep a close eye on children. Although crime is rarely reported in Guimaras, it’s still a good idea to discuss and negotiate transportation costs beforehand and check the freshness of fruits when buying to avoid being overcharged or buying spoiled goods, as some vendors may take advantage of clueless visitors. Traveling here is safe, but you should always be careful and alert.

Hidden Tourist Spot in Eastern Visayas

Linao Cave in Guiuan


Linao Cave is a beautiful semi-open cenote located on Calicoan Island in Brgy. Sulangan, Guiuan, Eastern Samar. Its name, "Linao," originated from the Waray-Waray word for "clear," which perfectly reflects its clear, crystal-blue waters. It is a unique natural wonder, perfect for swimming and exploring, with amazing stalagmite and stalactite structures visible inside.

Location of this
Hidden Gem

Brgy. Sulangan, Guiuan, Eastern Samar, Philippines

Ideal Time and Months to Visit

The best time to visit Linao Cave is during the summer months, from March to May, when the weather is ideal for swimming and beating the heat. You can also visit between September and October to experience the Calicoan Odyssey Waves, a local surfing event that showcases the island's vibrant surf culture. The cave's crystal-clear waters are perfect for a refreshing dip and for exploring its natural beauty. It’s recommended to go in the morning or afternoon when there's plenty of light. Avoid visiting at night or when it’s dark, as the cave can become difficult to navigate.

Discover Local Celebrations

Calicoan Odyssey Waves (National Surfing Competition)

The Calicoan Odyssey Waves is a surfing competition held at the famous ABCD surf break in Calicoan Island, Guiuan, Eastern Samar. It is a Grade 2 surf competition and music festival that brings together top surfers from around the country, with equal prizes for both men and women. Held every September or October, the week-long event celebrates the island's vibrant surf culture and growing surfing community.

Comfortable Places to Stay

Casa Patron Calicoan

Casa Patron Calicoan is a private vacation home on Calicoan Island in Guiuan, Eastern Samar. It is close to the beach and perfect for surfing, kayaking, and other activities. Guests can cook their own meals and explore nearby places using a guest booklet containing a list of nearby restaurants and activities provided by the host.

Taste the Local Flavors

Cali Island Grill

Craving grilled food like stuffed squid, fish, pork belly, or chicken? Cali Island Grill, located on Calicoan Island in Ngolos, Guiuan, Eastern Samar, serves up flavorful and affordable home-cooked meals. It’s the perfect place to enjoy island-inspired dishes with a local touch.

Tomorrow Land Grill and Restaurant

Tomorrow Land Grill and Restaurant serves a variety of silog meals, fresh seafood, chicken, and pork dishes packed with flavor. It’s a cozy spot to enjoy hearty Filipino meals in a laid-back setting. You can also meet friendly locals who may guide you on your Linao Cave trip.

Safety Assessment

Linao Cave in Guiuan, Eastern Samar, is considered a generally safe tourist destination, with no reported cases of scams or pickpocketing in the area. However, as with any trip, it’s still best to stay alert and keep your belongings secure. The cave itself can be slippery, so wearing proper footwear is essential. If you're planning to swim, a life jacket is recommended, especially for non-swimmers. It’s also strongly advised to explore the cave with a local tour guide to ensure safety and avoid accidents. Overall, Guiuan remains a peaceful and welcoming town for travelers.

Hidden Tourist Spot in Central Visayas

Nova Shell Museum in Panglao, Bohol


Do you love the sea and marine biology?
Come here at Nova Shell Museum where you'll be amazed by one of the largest and rarest collections of seashells in the Philippines. With a wide variety of shells, some of which are unique to the Philippines, this museum is an entertaining and educational place to learn about marine life.

Location of this
Hidden Gem

Teofilo Arcay St, Poblacion, Panglao, Bohol

Ideal Time and Months to Visit

Visit the museum between February and March, during the dry season when rainfall is low and humidity is more manageable, making it ideal for sightseeing and beach-hopping. Another great time to visit is during the last week of August, when a lively cultural celebration adds a unique local experience to your trip. Although August may bring occasional rain, the vibrant festival attract both locals and tourists alike. The best time of day to visit the museum is in the morning, ideally between 9:00 AM and 11:00 AM, when it’s less crowded. Morning light also enhances the shell displays, making it perfect for photos. After your visit, you’ll still have time in the afternoon to explore nearby attractions or unwind at the beach.

Discover Local Celebrations

Hudyaka sa Panglao Festival

In honor of St. Augustine, the town's patron saint, the Hudyaka sa Panglao Festival is held every August 28 as a celebration of gratitude, culture, and faith. This annual event highlights the community’s rich heritage and unity, as Panglao’s ten barangays compete in a dance showdown filled with colorful costumes, music, and cultural pride. The word "Hudyaka", meaning merrymaking in Cebuano, perfectly captures the joyful, week-long festive spirit that brings locals and visitors together in celebration.

Safety Assessment

The Nova Shell Museum is generally considered a very safe destination for both local and tourists. Panglao is a peaceful island town, and the area around the museum is quiet, with locals known for being warm and welcoming. You can safely walk around during the day, especially since the museum is near the town center and public market. Inside the museum, the environment is secure and monitored, but visitors should be careful around the shell displays to avoid damage. Always ask permission from the staff before touching or holding any of the seashells, as many are rare and fragile. For a comfortable visit, wear light clothing and carry small bills, especially if you plan to explore more of Panglao after your museum trip.
