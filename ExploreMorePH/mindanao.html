Welcome to Mindanao

Mindanao is the southernmost major island group of the Philippines, a land of untamed beauty, rich cultures, and awe-inspiring landscapes. Known as the country’s "Land of Promise," it boasts towering mountains like Mount Apo, pristine beaches such as Siargao’s Cloud 9, and lush rainforests teeming with wildlife. Beyond its natural wonders, Mindanao is a melting pot of diverse traditions, home to vibrant indigenous communities and flavorful cuisine. From the bustling cities of Davao and Cagayan de Oro to the tranquil waters of Camiguin and the enchanting river canyons of Surigao, Mindanao offers thrilling adventures, deep cultural immersion, and breathtaking scenery at every turn.

Hidden Tourist Spot in Davao Region

Philippine Eagle Center


World’s rarest and largest eagle? Visit the Philippine Eagle Center in Malagos, Davao City—a conservation hub for the critically endangered Pithecophaga jefferyi. Walk through lush forest trails, observe eagles up close, and learn about efforts to save this national bird. The center also shelters other wildlife like monkeys and owls, making it a perfect eco-educational trip.

Location of this
Hidden Gem

Malagos, Baguio District, Davao City, Philippines

Ideal Time and Months to Visit

The best time to visit the Philippine Eagle Center is from December to April, when the weather is dry and ideal for trekking. February and March are particularly pleasant, with cooler temperatures and fewer rain showers. Arrive early in the morning (8:00 AM–10:00 AM) to avoid crowds and enjoy a peaceful experience. Weekdays are less busy than weekends, offering a more relaxed visit.

Discover Local Celebrations

Kadayawan Festival

The Kadayawan Festival is Davao's vibrant celebration of culture, unity, and nature’s abundance, held every August. Highlights include the Indak-Indak sa Kadalanan, a lively street dance competition featuring colorful costumes and tribal rhythms, and the Pamulak sa Kadayawan, a dazzling floral float parade. Locals and tourists alike enjoy fresh fruits, traditional music, and the warm hospitality of Davaoeños during this joyous event.

Araw ng Davao

Araw ng Davao, celebrated every March 16, marks the city's founding with lively festivities, including trade fairs, concerts, and cultural showcases. The highlight is the Mutya ng Davao beauty pageant, which celebrates the grace and talent of Davaoeñas. Locals and visitors enjoy street parties, food festivals, and historical exhibits that honor the city’s rich heritage.

Comfortable Places to Stay

Malagos Garden Resort

Malagos Garden Resort is a serene nature escape in Davao, offering lush garden cottages, a vibrant butterfly sanctuary, and interactive chocolate-making tours. Visitors can unwind in its peaceful surroundings while exploring its eco-friendly attractions, including birdwatching and organic farms.

Taste the Local Flavors

Lola Abon’s Durian Candy

Lola Abon’s Durian Candy offers a deliciously sweet and creamy way to enjoy Davao’s famous—and pungent—fruit without the strong odor. Made from real durian pulp, these chewy candies capture the fruit’s rich flavor while being more approachable for first-timers. A must-try souvenir, they’re perfect for satisfying durian cravings or sharing a unique taste of Davao with friends back home.

Tiny Kitchen

Marang Flan at Tiny Kitchen is a luscious Davao delicacy that blends the sweet, custard-like flesh of marang with the velvety richness of classic flan. This tropical twist on the traditional dessert is perfectly complemented by a cup of thick, bittersweet sikwate (local hot chocolate), creating a heavenly flavor pairing. A must-try for foodies, it offers a unique taste of Davao’s bountiful harvests in every creamy bite.

Safety Assessment

The Philippine Eagle Center is generally a safe and well-managed eco-tourism site, with clearly marked trails and trained staff overseeing visitor activities. While the paths are well-maintained, some areas can become slippery during or after rain, so wearing sturdy footwear is advisable. The center is located in a secure, forested area, and there are no known scams or significant crime risks targeting tourists. Visitors can safely walk around the facility, though it’s best to stay on designated trails to avoid disturbing wildlife.

Hidden Tourist Spot in Northern Mindanao

Tinago Falls


Tucked in Iligan City, the "City of Majestic Waterfalls," Tinago Falls (literally "hidden falls") lives up to its name. A 500-step descent leads to a turquoise plunge pool surrounded by cliffs and vines. Swim under the 240-foot cascade or rent a bamboo raft to get closer to the roaring waters.

Location of this
Hidden Gem

Barangay Ditucalan, Iligan City, Lanao del Norte

Ideal Time and Months to Visit

The best time to visit Tinago Falls is from March to June, when the waters are clearest and the weather is driest for a safe descent. Weekdays are ideal to avoid crowds, especially during April and May, the peak summer months. For the best lighting and serene atmosphere, arrive early in the morning (7:00–10:00 AM). Avoid rainy months (July–October) when trails can be slippery and currents stronger.

Discover Local Celebrations

Iligan Diyandi Festival

The Iligan Diyandi Festival, held every September, is a vibrant celebration honoring St. Michael the Archangel, featuring colorful street dances, lively music, and dazzling fireworks. A highlight is the Hudyaka sa Suba (River Float Competition), where elaborately decorated barges parade along the Iligan River. Locals and visitors alike join the revelry, enjoying cultural performances, food fairs, and the warm hospitality of Iligan City.

Iligan Waterfall Adventure

This eco-tourism initiative lets adventurers explore Iligan’s 20+ stunning waterfalls, including Tinago Falls, through guided tours and thrilling zip-lining experiences. Perfect for nature lovers, the tours emphasize sustainability and local culture, offering insights into each waterfall’s history and ecosystem. Whether you're swimming in turquoise pools or rappelling down cascades, it’s a must-do for thrill-seekers.

Comfortable Places to Stay

Maria Cristina Hotel

This budget-friendly hotel offers clean, no-frills accommodations with the bonus of a refreshing outdoor pool—perfect for cooling off after a day of waterfall hopping. Located just 15 minutes from Tinago Falls, it provides easy access to Iligan’s top nature spots without breaking the bank. While simple, it delivers reliable comfort with friendly service, making it ideal for adventure-focused travelers.

Taste the Local Flavors

Cheding’s Peanuts

These crunchy, garlicky roasted peanuts are an iconic Iligan pasalubong, packed with bold flavor and perfect for snacking on road trips. Made with locally grown peanuts and a secret spice blend, they've been a favorite for generations. Don't leave Iligan without grabbing a bag (or three)!

Ams Chicken Haus, Inc.

This beloved local chain serves up juicy, garlic-roasted chicken that's perfectly crispy on the outside and tender inside, with a signature savory glaze. Known for their budget-friendly meals, they offer hearty rice bowls, grilled specialties, and homemade-style Filipino sides like chopsuey and lumpia. With its casual, family-friendly vibe and quick service, it's a top spot for a satisfying meal after exploring Iligan's waterfalls.

Modes of Transportation

Airplane

Fly to CDO (1.5 hrs), then take a bus/van to Iligan (2–3 hrs). No direct flights to Iligan.

Estimated Price: ₱2,500–₱5,000 (one-way from Manila to Laguindingan Airport, CDO)

Bus

Regular air-conditioned buses (e.g., Rural Transit) take ~3–4 hours from CDO or ~8–10 hours from Davao. Drops off at Iligan City terminals; ride a jeepney/tricycle to Tinago Falls (30 mins).

Estimated Price: ₱800–₱1,200 (one-way from Cagayan de Oro/Davao)

Motorcycle

Rent a habal-habal (motorcycle taxi) for a scenic but bumpy ride through mountain roads. Ideal for solo travelers with light luggage.

Estimated Price: ₱300–₱500 (one-way from CDO, 2–3 hours)
